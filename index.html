FAQ

Common Questions

Get answers to frequently asked questions about FMCSA compliance

Yes, under 49 USC § 14708, every interstate household goods motor carrier must offer shippers the option to use arbitration to settle disputes involving loss/damage claims and service charges. This is a federal requirement, not optional.

No, tariffs are not filed with FMCSA, STB, or DOT. However, you are legally required to maintain a published tariff in your office and make it available for inspection upon request. You must also reference your tariff on your Bill of Lading.

Penalties can be severe. Operating without a proper tariff can result in civil penalties up to $11,200 per violation. Charging rates not in your tariff can result in criminal penalties including fines and up to 2 years imprisonment. FMCSA can also order refunds to consumers and suspend your authority.

BOC-3 (Blanket of Coverage) is a form filed with FMCSA that designates process agents in every state where you operate. Per 49 CFR Part 366, this is required before FMCSA will grant your operating authority. A process agent is your legal representative who receives court papers and government notices on your behalf.
